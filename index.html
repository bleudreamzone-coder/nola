05 · The Pharmacology No One Explained
Your medications are talking to each other.
Nobody told you what they're saying.

The average American adult over 65 takes 4.5 prescription medications. In Louisiana, where chronic illness rates are among the highest in the nation, that number climbs higher. Every medication passes through your liver's CYP450 enzyme system — and when two drugs compete for the same enzyme, one gets amplified to dangerous levels while the other fails to work at all. Most people are never told this. Most prescription labels don't mention it.

BLEU's safety engine screens 302,000 interaction pairs across 5 pharmacological layers: CYP450 Phase I enzymes, UGT Phase II enzymes, P-glycoprotein transporters, pharmacodynamic overlap, and route-specific modifiers. It checks cannabis interactions, supplement conflicts, and the herbs that 40% of Americans take without telling their doctor. It runs in seconds. It's free. And it flags the conversation your doctor's 12-minute appointment window often doesn't have time for.

are completely preventable — with information.
06 · The Loneliness Epidemic
Loneliness kills faster
than obesity.

In 2023 the U.S. Surgeon General issued a formal advisory declaring loneliness a public health epidemic. The data behind it: social isolation increases the risk of premature death by 26%. It raises the risk of heart disease by 29%, stroke by 32%, and dementia by 50%. These are not feelings. They are physiological events — elevated cortisol, suppressed immune function, systemic inflammation — driven by the absence of the one thing human beings are biologically built to require: each other.

Jazz Bird NOLA's community wellness circles are a clinical response to a clinical problem.
